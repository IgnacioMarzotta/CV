Profile

The code I'm most proud of isn't the most elegant I've written, but the one that someone used to do something they couldn't do before.

More than a programmer, I consider myself a technical problem solver, with a theoretical foundation in engineering and the pragmatism of having worked on real-world projects from the early stages of my career. I understand that code must be clean, but above all, it must provide real value and work for the end user.

My career path

A diferencia de la ruta tradicional, mi carrera comenzó en paralelo a mis estudios universitarios. Integrarme tempranamente a poryectos reales (Cuatro Coigües) y startups (Forcast) me enseñó lecciones que no se aprenden en el aula, como la importancia de los tiempos de entrega, la comunicación con usuarios y clientes no técnicos, y la resiliencia para resolver errores en producción.

Today, as a qualified Engineer, my goal is to merge that operational experience with best practices in architecture and scalability, seeking to integrate into structured teams where I can contribute and continue specializing.

Philosophy

I am guided by three key principles:

  • Technical Pragmatism: Look for the most efficient solution, not necessarily the most complex one.
  • Transparent Communication: Document what you learn, raise your hand when you don't know something, and answer when you do know it.
  • Adaptability:Technology changes rapidly, and the willingness to learn a new framework or language is more valuable than clinging to just one.

Interests & Hobbies

Outside of work, I'm an avid cyclist and believe in mental and physical balance as the foundation for better thinking. I also enjoy personal projects in electronics, virtual reality, and keeping up with the evolution of the technology and cloud ecosystem.
