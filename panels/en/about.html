Profile

I deeply believe in the impact that good ideas can have when combined with technology and collaboration. My goal is to provide solutions that not only work, but also improve people's lives and generate real progress.

More than a programmer, I consider myself a technical problem solver, with an approach that combines the theoretical basis of engineering with the pragmatism acquired in fieldwork, because I understand that code must be clean, but above all, it must provide real value and work for the end user.

My career path

Unlike the traditional path, my career began alongside my university studies. Early integration into freelance development teams (Cuatro Coigües) and startups (Forcast) taught me lessons not learned in the classroom, such as the importance of deadlines, communication with non-technical users and clients, and resilience in resolving production errors.

Today, as a qualified Engineer, my goal is to merge that operational experience with best practices in architecture and scalability, seeking to integrate into structured teams where I can contribute and continue specializing.

Philosophy

I am guided by three key principles:

  • Technical Pragmatism: Look for the most efficient solution, not necessarily the most complex one.
  • Transparent Communication: Document what you learn, raise your hand when you don't know something, and answer when you do know it.
  • Adaptability:Technology changes rapidly, and the willingness to learn a new framework or language is more valuable than clinging to just one.

Interests & Hobbies

Outside of the IDE, I believe in mental and physical balance. I'm an enthusiast of cycling and virtual reality, spaces where I recharge my energy, and I also enjoy exploring personal electronics projects and keeping up with the evolution of the Web3 and Cloud ecosystem.
